Best VPN for Streaming Netflix & BBC iPlayer Abroad

UPDATED 2026·6 MIN READ

Nothing kills a long flight or a quiet hotel evening like opening Netflix and finding half your watchlist missing because you've crossed a border. The fix is a VPN — but not every VPN handles streaming equally well.

Streaming platforms invest heavily in detecting and blocking VPN traffic, because their content licenses are tied to specific regions. That means the VPN market is full of services that technically "work" but get blocked within days, or that unblock a library only at a crawl. For travelers who just want their usual shows to work like they do at home, reliability matters far more than a marketing page full of server-count claims.

What actually matters for streaming while traveling

  • Speed under load. Streaming in HD or 4K needs a stable, fast connection — a VPN that constantly buffers isn't worth using.
  • Consistency against blocks. Some VPNs get their servers blacklisted by streaming platforms and stay that way for weeks.
  • Server choice. You'll want to connect to a server in the specific country whose library you're after — home, or elsewhere.

How to stream your home library while abroad

  1. Install the VPN app before you leave — some app stores restrict VPN downloads once you're already in certain countries.
  2. Connect to a server located in your home country (or the country whose library you want).
  3. Open your streaming app as usual. If a title doesn't load, try switching to a different server in the same country — not all servers perform identically.

Surfshark as a lower-cost alternative

Surfshark is a capable streaming option too, and its unlimited-device policy is handy if you're sharing one plan across a family's worth of tablets and phones. It's the better pick if cost and device count matter more to you than squeezing out the absolute best streaming speeds. See our best budget VPN for travelers guide for more on that angle.

A quick note on legality

Using a VPN to access your own paid subscriptions while traveling is generally considered a gray area by streaming services' terms rather than something with legal consequences for you as a viewer. Still, it's worth knowing that platforms can and do interrupt access if they detect VPN use, so treat it as a convenience rather than a guarantee.

Frequently asked questions

Why does Netflix block some VPNs?

Streaming services license content by region, so they actively detect and block known VPN server addresses to enforce those agreements.

Will a VPN slow down my streaming quality?

There's some overhead from encryption, but a fast VPN on a nearby server keeps that impact small enough that you generally won't notice it during playback.
